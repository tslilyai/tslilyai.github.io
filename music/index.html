Music

Since I can remember, music has been a large part of my life: as a baby, I would fall asleep to Beethoven and wake up to my sister’s practicing. I picked up the piano at age 4 and the violin at age 6. In my musical career, I have been extremely fortunate to have learned from and played for some of my musical heros: my teachers, Li Lin and Lynn Chang; the Parker, Pacifica, and Borromeo Quartets; Hilary Hahn, and many more.

Music has given me opportunities to travel around the world and perform in 9 different countries. My most patriotic act to date was as concertmistress of the inaugural National Youth Orchestra in 2013, where we played under the baton of Valery Gergiev with bright red pants and star-spangled converse sneakers in the BBC proms.

I am currently getting my PhD minor in violin performance, and participating in the vibrant chamber music communities at both Harvard and MIT. I also take guilty pleasure in arranging my favorite non-classical songs for many violins and replicating myself to record these arrangements.
