About Me

I am currently a third-year PhD student at MIT as part of the PDOS group, advised by Professor Frans Kaashoek. My research interests broadly include systems ensuring data privacy, performance and scalability in systems, and the application of formal methods to real-life software.

Besides research, I enjoy playing violin, reading, rock climbing, and exploring the world around me. I am always free to sightread chamber music!

History

I spent 2017-18 abroad as a Fulbright Research Fellow at MPI-SWS, where I was advised by Professor Peter Druschel. I graduated in 2017 from Harvard University, where I received my Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees in Computer Science and was advised by Professor Eddie Kohler.

While I’ve remained in the Cambridge/Massachusetts area for over half a decade now, my hometown in the SF Bay Area will always have a piece of my heart.
